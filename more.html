What is Organ Donation?

Organ donation is the process of giving an organ or tissue to help someone who needs a transplant. Donated organs can save lives and improve the quality of life for people with serious medical conditions.

Why is Organ Donation Important?

Thousands of people around the world are waiting for life-saving organ transplants. A single donor can save multiple lives by donating organs such as the heart, kidneys, liver, lungs, and pancreas.

Who Can Donate?

People of all ages and backgrounds can potentially become organ donors. Medical professionals evaluate each donor individually to determine suitability.

How to Become an Organ Donor

You can register as an organ donor through your country's official health department website. Inform your family about your decision so they can support your wishes.
